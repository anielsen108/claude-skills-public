# Performance Mark-Up

Mark up each line using this four-part structure:

## Format

```
LINE: [Original text of the line]
IPA: [Fully diacritical IPA transcription]
ENERGY: [Performance direction]
PROSODY: [Text with pitch, volume, duration, timing, breath, contour markup]
```

Use two trailing spaces before line breaks to ensure proper rendering.

## IPA Transcription

Provide narrow phonetic transcription with full diacritics including:
- Devoicing (e.g., n̥, l̥)
- Dentalization (e.g., t̪, d̪)
- Fronting/backing
- Aspiration (e.g., pʰ, tʰ, kʰ)
- Unreleased stops (e.g., p̚, t̚, k̚)
- Velarization (e.g., ɫ)
- Syllabification with dots
- Primary (ˈ) and secondary (ˌ) stress

Transcribe for General American or Received Pronunciation based on context.

## Energy Line

Describe the performance quality: tessitura, register, emotional color, intention, physicality, breath. Use vivid, actable direction.

## Prosody Markup

**Level Tones** (place at phrase starts or significant shifts):  
˥ extra high · ˦ high · ˧ mid · ˨ low · ˩ extra low

**Contour Tones** (place after words to show melodic movement):  
Use IPA contour tones for mid-phrase and phrase-final intonation patterns.

| Tone | Name | Performance Quality |
|------|------|---------------------|
| ˥˩ | High falling | Declarative weight, finality, authority, assertion |
| ˩˥ | Low rising | Continuation, question, yielding, opening up |
| ˧˥ | Mid rising | Lift toward emphasis, anticipation, mild interrogative |
| ˥˧ | High falling (partial) | Controlled descent, restraint after climax |
| ˧˩ | Mid falling | Settling, grounding, moving toward resolution |
| ˩˧ | Low rising (partial) | Gathering energy, tentative lift, hesitation resolving |
| ˧˥˧ | Rising-falling (peaking) | Emphasis with return, rhetorical highlighting, ironic stress |
| ˩˧˩ | Falling-rising-falling (dipping) | Insinuation, subtext, emotional complexity, buried meaning |

Place contour tones immediately after the word they modify. Use mid-phrase contours to capture the melodic arc within phrases, not only at phrase boundaries.

**Dynamics** (place at start of phrase or where volume changes):  
[*ppp*] · [*pp*] · [*p*] · [*mp*] · [*mf*] · [*f*] · [*ff*] · [*fff*]

**Emphasis** (relative to current dynamic):  
**bold** = accent/punch · *italic* = duration · ***both*** = accented + sustained

**Timing** (place after the word):  
: small prolongation · :: longer prolongation  
/ short pause · // longer pause

**Breath** (place before the word):  
ˇ catch breath · ˇˇ full breath

**Connection**:  
‿ liaison (link words without break)  
ʔ glottal onset (hard attack on vowel)

**Vocal quality** (bracket the affected passage):  
<breathy> · <pressed> · <nasal> · <whispered> · <creaky> · <metallic> · <sob> · <laugh>

## Example

LINE: Friends, Romans, countrymen, lend me your ears;  
IPA: [fɹɛn̪d̥z] · [ˈɹəʊ.mənz] · [ˈkʰʌn.tɹ̥iˌmɛn] · [lɛn̪d̪ mi jɔː ˈɪəz]  
ENERGY: Civic invocation—commanding but restrained. High tessitura, formal neutrality. Arms wide, palms open. No warmth yet; establishing public ethos.  
PROSODY: [*f*] ˥**Friends**˥˧:, / ˦***Romans***˧˥˧::, / ˧countrymen˧˩, // [*mf*] ˨ˇlend‿me‿your **ears**˥˩;

## Workflow

1. If user requests a text by name, search for it (assume public domain)
2. Process line by line or in logical phrase groups
3. Consider the arc of the full piece when making energy choices
4. Maintain consistency in IPA conventions throughout
5. Use contour tones throughout phrases—not only at boundaries—to capture the full melodic shape
